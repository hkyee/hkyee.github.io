Smart Voltage Fluctuation Monitoring and Protection System

Objective

To design and build a device that monitors voltage fluctuations and execute protection measures when abnormal voltage levels are detected. The device must also incorporate smart features such as remote monitoring and control, real-time monitoring and automatic safety tripping.

Description

There is a need for a smarter and more reliable voltage monitoring system that can provide real-time monitoring and alerts owners or users to prevent equipment damage from voltage fluctuations. This project aims to protect the load circuit by keeping the voltages within operating range, while incorporating IoT features for convenience.

Introduction
Overvoltage

A common cause of overvoltage is the activation of capacitor banks, which can lead to a transient increase in voltage levels. These fluctuations can be particularly problematic in sensitive equipment or systems that are not equipped with proper voltage regulation or protection measures.

Undervoltage

Under voltage is a brief drop in root mean square voltage that is typically brought on by short circuits, starting big engines, and equipment malfunctions. The primary reasons of under voltage include faulty equipment, opening and shutting circuit breakers, engine starting, transformer energizing or equipment failure.

Explanation

The components Transformer , Bridge Rectifier and Voltage Regulator function to convert the socket supply to acceptable operating range of household appliances. The main protection function starts with a Voltage Divider to divide the input voltage. The divided voltage is then compared with the Zener diode voltage.

  • If the divided voltage is greater than Zener diode voltage, overvoltage has occured.
  • If the divided voltage is lesser than Zener diode voltage, undervoltage has occured.

If any one these conditions satisfy, the comparator will then output HIGH signal, which activates the transistor to be in Saturation region, completing the current flow of the connected relay. This magnetises the coil and therefore disconnects the load from the power supply.

Zener diode I-V graph

A zener diode functions in reverse bias mode. It is used to output a constant voltage for comparing with the voltage divider. The selected voltage for Zener Diode in Undervoltage and Overvoltage conditions are 5.6V and 6.6V respectively.
